About the Lab

Solving the space debris problem through autonomy

SSA Lab is an academic lab in the Grainger School of Engineering at the University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign. We focus primarily on solving the space debris problem by addressing critical challenges in missions with rendezvous and proximity operations.

Our work spans the entire lifecycle of in-orbit servicing missions ‚Äî from preliminary trajectory design to real-time autonomous guidance during the final approach. We combine classical astrodynamics with modern AI techniques including reinforcement learning and convex optimization.

We collaborate with partners across academia and industry, including MIT, the Australian Centre for Field Robotics, Astroscale, and JPL, to push the boundaries of what's achievable in autonomous space operations.
